Air Quality and Your Health

The air in your area can affect everyone, especially older adults and young children who may have trouble breathing when pollution levels are high. By staying aware of your local air quality, you can help protect your loved ones and your community from health risks linked to air pollution.

Common Air Pollutants:

  • Nitrogen Dioxide (NO₂): Can irritate the lungs and make breathing harder, especially for people with asthma or respiratory issues.
  • Nitric Oxide (NO): An invisible gas that can turn into nitrogen dioxide and contribute to fog and poor air quality.
  • Particulate Matter (PM₁₀): Tiny particles of dust, dirt, or liquid droplets in the air that can enter your lungs and cause health problems.
  • Carbon Monoxide (CO): A colorless, odorless gas that limits the amount of oxygen your body receives, often coming from car exhaust or burning fuels.
